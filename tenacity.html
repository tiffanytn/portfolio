OVERVIEW

For our senior capstone project, my team and I had the fortune to work with Professor Kurt Squire and his Tenacity research group at the Connected Learning Lab, a design lab at UCI dedicated to learner-centered technology. We were tasked with challenge of reimagining and improving their existing mobile and Apple Watch application, Tenacity, geared towards younger users and novices to the concept of mindfulness, namely teenagers ages 13-15.

Tenacity was created by developers and researchers at the Connected Learning Lab as a digital wellness application that centers around improving self-regulation and attention skills, while also providing stress relief, through mindful breathing techniques (as an introductory to practicing mindfulness). The issue with their app? It wasn't engaging enough, according to their current users and test participants. Our job? To breathe new air into this application by providing an engaging and meaningful experience, while also addressing existing ux pain points (detailed in our testing and research).

My contributions to this project included being the UI/UX Design lead (basically doing all things design-related from wireframing, designing mockups, creating slide decks, prototyping in Invision, and designing our showcase posters). Since researchers in the Tenacity team and lab had conducted extensive qualitative user research and collected quantitative data—and much of the interviewing were restricted for me to be involved in due to IRB and privacy reasons—much of the user research I did was comprised of sitting with Tenacity researchers to go through and synthesize their existing user data.
