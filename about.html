What is my design story?


As you can tell by this point of the website, my name is Tiffany and I am full-spectrum designer (or product designer) based in Southern California. Currently, I am a senior at the University of California, Irvine studying Informatics and Human-Computer Interaction.

Initially, design was understood as strictly aesthetics and visuals and so, my beginnings in design reflected that (meaning, I did a lot of graphic design). I later stumbled into the world of human-centered design and user-experience and have been tinkering and and learning how to use design to translate problems and needs into opportunities since.

I seek to understand and work within the intersection between design, technology, and psychology to ultimately craft meaningful and evidence-driven experiences and solutions that meet user and business needs. I have grown an interest in product design and consider myself a generalist; I like getting into a bit of everything: user research, ux design, and visual design as it allows me to carry and take into consideration my understanding of the problem and user’s need from beginning to end.

Whe I am not designing, you will most likely find me trying my hand at film photography, cooking (this is a new one), and curating playlists on Spotify. I have also had the fortune of revamping and directing a design-oriented, student organization at UC Irvine called Design at UCI! So if you are a designer and looking to give back and share your wisdom to some aspiring designers, that’s a great place to start!

If I have sparked your curiousity enough for you to consider chatutting with me on anything, I would say (in the words of a close friend) don’t be a stranger!
